  • Mexico

The New Global activities in Mexico build up largely on the activities and company and stakeholder networks of the ECO-Hostel project in Viente de Noviembre, a village in Campeche, Mexico. Aalto Lab Mexico’s ECO-Hostel project has been running from 2012, and this concept derives from two opportunity areas identified in the field: Empowering economic activity of the locals through community (eco) tourism, and generating infrastructure that is specifically designed to meet the local climatologic conditions and cultural traditions.

Main activities during 2014

31.3-21.3: Field trip

  • Project-based learning
  • Start-ups
  • Connected collaborative projects and programs

Finally, the on-going development of new economic networks and business potential to support a growing eco-system between the regions, for example:

  • Incubators
  • University (Alumni) and Non university based program networks (associations etc)
  • Start-up community.

One of the central outcomes of the field trip in general is to identify and clarify those parties that will be available for both the summer workshop in June, and the Mexican cultural and business event in September.

9-13.6: Mexican companies workshop

The June event will be a two-week (2 – 13.6) workshop that is supported by the CS Summer workshop program. There are several parts to the workshop, part of which focuses on the Mexican companies. This part of the workshop will focus on student, staff and expert private sector engagement with companies via scenarios and case studies to gain a better understanding of what are the business development potentials, between the regions. The workshop participants will potentially include (from Helsinki and Mexico):

  • Government individuals and departments
  • Students and Staff
  • Private sector experts
  • Companies of different size

1 – 30.9: Mexican Month - Sponsored by the Mexican Embassy

This will be an opportunity to develop a week long Business Week that corresponds with a cultural event. This will benefit those interested in this region through the business week to include an understanding of what is culturally relevant, interesting and important. The business week should include those interested in participating from both Mexico and Finland through showcasing ideas and having the opportunity to discuss each other’s projects and products etc.

Main partners

The partners will include some previous participants from Aalto LAB projects, Summer workshop 2013 eg: Tech de Monterrey Uni, UNAM, Palmo, IDEO, FCG and new partners that are currently working together on the Eco-hostel project in April and others to be confirmed.

According to the World Bank, 28.2% of the Tanzanian population lived below the national poverty line in 2012. However, according to Carlos Lopez, executive secretary to the Economic Commission for Africa, most African countries will be middle income by 2040 (Africa Renewal, 2013). This means that a large proportion of those currently below the poverty line will become middle income in the not too distant future. One of New Global’s guiding principles is to respond to these inevitable challenges through commercial, frugal innovations that serve the local current and emerging needs

Main activities

Aalto University has been active in Tanzania for many years. Research on Base of the Pyramid markets and construction has been carried out since 2009, and a multi-disciplinary master’s studio has had its focus on Dar es Salaam for two subsequent years. Projects that have been completed by the students together with local communities are in the fields of sanitation, waste management, marketing, affordable housing, resettlement, flooding and urban planning. The sanitation project from 2013 is currently being developed further and executed during 2014.

The New Global project is looking for companies with new ideas and openings in the fields that Aalto has worked in the area before, or in new fields. The New Global project is also looking for companies open towards building on the projects that students have already initiated and worked on, in order to spin them further on with the help of existing local networks.

Majority of Fortune 500 companies are now examining frugal innovation and its role in the knowledge economy. India is home for several significant frugal innovations (known as “Jugaad” in India) that have been scaled up to large-scale businesses. Examples of such are Tata Nano, Aravind Eye Clinic, and GE’s handheld electrocardiogram machine that later evolved into a reverse innovation since it was adopted to western markets.

Main activities during 2014

In the New Global India site the first year is dedicated to setting up activities and creating partnerships. This will be achieved by active presence. The following field trips are being prepared for 2014:

  • 18-28.4: Business with impact – Co-creation Journey to India: Delhi, Patna and villages in State of Bihar.
  • 6-10.4: NG team will be part of Aalto delegation to Mumbai
  • 12-14.6: Participating to Design for Sustainable Well-being and Empowerment conference in Bangalore
  • 29.9-3.10: Field trip and Nordic Innovation camp in Mumbai
  • Nov/Dec. The New Global innovation camp either in Kanpur of Roorke (date TBD)

Partnering is the key

In India the New Global is presently partnering with Team Finland, and the local site manager Sujil Kodathoor, based in India, is mapping candidates for local partners. Moreover, The New Global is involved in close research collaboration with the Indian Institutes of Technologies (IIT) network.

There is already evidence that western firms are achieving frugal innovations through local R&D subsidiaries located in emerging countries. The New Global project team will work together with Finnish organizations to help them address the potential of frugal innovations and thereby encouraging firms to join NG activities in India.
