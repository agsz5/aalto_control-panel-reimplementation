People

Minna Halme
Professor, project director
minna.halme@aalto.fi

Minna Halme is professor of management at Aalto University School of Business. Her research focuses on inclusive business models for poverty alleviation and sustainability management in organizations. She has worked with a number of European research projects and headed several national ones. She teaches Business & Sustainability in Aalto's master's programs and executive MBA courses in Finland and internationally. She cooperates with companies in several research projects.

Sara Lindeman
Project manager
sara.lindeman@aalto.fi

Sara Lindeman is an expert on inclusive business. Her research focuses on market creation in low-income world majority contexts. Sara has worked closely with Finnish companies on inclusive innovation in the fields of renewable energy, forestry and ICT.

Teija Lehtonen
Aalto Global Impact's Director
teija.lehtonen@aalto.fi

Teija Lehtonen, M.Sc. (Econ) works as a Director of the Aalto Global Impact strategic initiative at the Aalto University. Her professional career of over 25 years covers leadership, business development, human resource and financial management in private sector and international organizations. Her long-term field experience covers India, Sri Lanka, Nepal, Afghanistan, Abkhazia/Georgia, Sierra Leone and Ivory Coast. She is currently promoting inclusive innovations and business at the Base of the Pyramid (BOP) market through various networks.

Petri Allekotte
Business Engagement Manager
petri.allekotte@aalto.fi

Petri Allekotte is responsible of corporate engagement into Aalto’s business development innovation projects in developing high potential countries. Petri is also responsible of developing Aalto’s coordination of its strategic corporate partnership program and fostering international social entrepreneurship through Aalto Global Impact’s projects. He has extensive career in senior strategic business development leadership positions in globally operating technology, wellbeing and service sectors corporates.

Tatu Lyytinen
PhD Candidate
tatu.lyytinen@aalto.fi

Tatu Lyytinen is PhD candidate at Aalto University School of Business Department of Management and International Business. His research interest includes international business, inclusive business models, and business ecosystems. He is interested in connecting scientific and practical knowledge in management of business in emerging markets. Tatu has worked with international politics, international business and applied research especially related to Latin America and Africa.

Susu Nousala
Research fellow
susu.nousala@aalto.fi

Susu Nousala (PhD) Currently a project-based learning researcher/teacher in Aalto University’s Creative Sustainability program, she is a Research Fellow at GAMUT research group, University of Melbourne, and a co-director of Kororoit Institute (KI) Australia. Previously a Research Fellow at RMIT University College of Design and Social Context, and an Australian Research Fellow/Project Manager for an EU FP5 for process improvement for SMEs, the Cost-Worth project. Also invited scientist for the UNEP foresight Process on Emerging Environmental Issues 2012. Research interests include: social complex adaptive systems. To date - chaired international symposiums/conferences, international keynote, authored over 50 refereed journal, conference papers, book chapters.

Helena Sandman
Country responsible researcher
helena.sandman@aalto.fi

M.Sc. Helena Sandman’s research is on affordable housing for rapidly growing communities using architecture as an empowering tool. She is founding member of Ukumbi NGO providing architecture as a means of development and a partner in Hollmén Reuter Sandman Architects Ltd. Helena has been teaching multidisciplinary student teams in project based slum upgrading since 2009 and is lecturing regularly world wide. 

Antti Hellgren
Project administrator
antti.hellgren@aalto.fi

Antti Hellgren is an aspiring professional in the field of international business at the Base of Pyramid. In Aalto University Antti is presently finalizing his master’s thesis on corporate-NGO partnership opportunities at the Chinese BoP. Moreover, Antti has extensively explored the developing world in Asia with his camera, and also spent 3 years studying and working in South Korea and China.
